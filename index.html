CT and X-ray fluorescence (XRF)

XRF is an excellent technique to obtain the missing information on chemical properties that CT does not provide.

Theory:

  • X-ray from primary source ejects electron and creates vacancy
  • Vacancy is filled by electron from outer shell
  • Characteristic X-ray is emitted

M. Boone et al. 2011. 3D Petrography of a Cu-Ba Mineralization by Combining µCT and µXRF. IAMG 2011, 492–493.

Practical example: How can we combine SEM, EDS, FIB/SEM and X-ray Tomography

Goal?

Workflow for structural and chemical characterization of a representative volume of a material

Different techniques have different properties Goal is to try to exploit the advantages of each technique

SEM: High resolution / 2D / No true chemical information

EDS: Chemical information / 2D

FIB nano-tomography: High resolution / 3D / Destructive / Very small volumes (is this representative?)

µCT: 3D / Non destructive / Large samples possible / No true chemical information / Resolution depends on sample size (max around 0.5 µm)
